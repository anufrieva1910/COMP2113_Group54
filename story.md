# The Story of Kyon Khryseos
## Background Lore — Cerberus's Cave

*This file exists as a reference for the development team.
It is the full narrative that the player uncovers piece by piece
through wall art and diary entries across all ten floors.*

---

Cheers and applause echo through the walls of this room. People
laugh, pray, and receive blessings from their almighty Canine.
Their god, named Kyon Khryseos and known as The Golden Hound,
always walked among his people — the same way his people walked
beside him. He was not a god who expected offerings and prayers
from a distance. He was one of them. This room was once a
celebration hall, where followers gathered to grow closer to
their god and to each other, sharing advice, love, and care.
The Golden Hound was gentle, warm, and giving. He always made
time for his followers, offering food and blessings. Equity and
love ruled this place. He was the protector of the heavens — a
god that represented life, peace, and unity.

Celebrations were held often, and people looked forward to every
one of them. Felines and canines lived in peace, respecting their
differences and standing stronger together. The brawn of the
canines, the wisdom of the felines.

At some point, things began to change. Celebrations were held
less often. The Canine would leave ceremonies earlier than usual.
Something shifted in his gaze. A few people noticed. Most did
not. It is difficult to notice subtle changes when the room is
full of laughter. The Golden Hound did everything in his power
to stay present and look after his people — but when the moment
came, when he showed signs of struggle and pain, most of his
followers did not put in the effort to see it.

He began spending more and more time alone. Scholars and warriors
started noticing The Canine digging deeper into the ground,
carving out a new room — deeper, darker. He would retreat there,
then return for ceremonies as if nothing had changed. Some
followers placed an offering table in that new room, hoping to
lift his spirit.

Days passed. Ceremonies grew rare. Warriors reported sightings
of The Golden Hound standing at the edge of the tallest cliff in
the village. But he was not alone.

Void black, faintly laced with shimmering gold, with desperate
and mischievous hazel eyes — Bastet, the Feline Goddess. She had
once been exiled from the village by the Canine God himself, cast
out as her greed grew beyond all limits. She was powerful, but
she did not know restraint. Warriors stood frozen — terrified and
confused in equal measure. Were the two gods joining forces again?
Or was something far worse unfolding?

The people grew uneasy. Bastet began appearing more frequently,
convincing the nation that The Golden Hound had willingly handed
her his divine duties. Meanwhile, the cave grew deeper. Each new
room was darker than the last — filled with chaos, pain, and
anguish. The Canine god was losing himself. He was no longer the
being his people knew. Darkness, rage, and a power he could not
control consumed him whole.

*"Hear my call, Great Cerberus — protector of the hell gates,
divine beast of the underworld! Your holy duties end now."*

The voice of the Goddess burrowed into the mind of the beast, as
it slowly lost what remained of its sanity. The Golden Hound,
once protector of the heavens, was reborn as the almighty beast
of the underworld.

---

Years passed. Conflict spread across the nation. Felines and
canines were divided. With the emergence of Cerberus, canines
became synonymous with threat and fear. They were believed to be
cruel beasts — dangerous by nature. Warrior cats trained their
entire lives to protect the feline nation, and the bravest among
them were sent to face Cerberus itself. To this day, none had
ever returned from the underground dungeons.

It did not frighten our warrior. He was strong, and he knew the
power he carried within him. Bastet rose as ruler of the felines,
driving canines from their homes — leaving them homeless, starving,
and alone. Scholars attempted to investigate the cave and uncover
the origin of Cerberus, but every one of them vanished without a
trace the moment they stepped inside. No bodies were ever found.
They simply disappeared.

The cat warrior was the first to uncover the truth. He conquered
each floor one by one — defeating the enemies summoned by the
beast, solving ancient puzzles, and piecing together the history
of this place through wall art left behind by the scholars who had
vanished before him. The deeper he went, the more fragmented and
desperate the carvings became. But he was sharp. He read between
the lines.

The battle was brutal. Neither opponent was willing to yield. But
as the final strike landed, something shifted. The dark force
slowly drained from the beast's body, leaving behind something
small and trembling — a frightened puppy. The Golden Hound had
been possessed all along. As the evil force departed its exhausted
form, what remained was fragile, scared, and innocent.

The truth was out. The nation rose against the Feline Goddess.
Felines and canines found their way back to each other. With every
prayer, every act of love and care, The Golden Hound grew stronger.
The nation healed — slowly, imperfectly, but together.

Yet the damage was not forgotten. Not all canines accepted the
apology. Wounds that deep do not close easily.

What does the future hold for this nation?...

---

Hazel eyes emerged from the fog, meeting the gaze of the canines
who had suffered at her hands. A crowd full of rage stood before
her. She did not look away.

*"Was it I who betrayed you — or was it the people who once swore
to protect you?"*
