ENDING_START
The cave falls quiet after the final blow. Not the quiet of
emptiness — the quiet of something finally finished.

You stand over the place where Cerberus fell. The air is still.
The dark force lifts slowly, like smoke thinning after a fire,
and what remains on the ground is small. Trembling. A puppy,
barely older than a few months, curled tight against itself,
eyes squeezed shut.

You sheathe your weapon.

The puppy opens its eyes. Hazel. Wide. It looks at you the way
something looks at you when it has forgotten what safety feels
like.

It opens its mouth. Its voice comes out broken, like it hasn't
been used in a very long time.

Kyon: "...where... where am I..."

You crouch down. You keep your hands visible.

Warrior: "You're safe. It's over."

Kyon: "I couldn't... I couldn't stop it... she kept talking and
I couldn't make it stop... everything was so loud..."

Warrior: "I know. I read the walls. All of them."

A long silence. The puppy looks at its own paws, as if seeing
them for the first time.

Kyon: "Did I... did I hurt people...?"

You don't answer immediately. You owe him the truth.

Warrior: "Yes. But that wasn't you. That was what she turned
you into."

Kyon: "...is there a difference?"

Warrior: "There will be. When they know the truth, there will be."

You reach out slowly. The puppy flinches, then doesn't. You
place your hand on its head, gently.

Kyon: "...I had a name. Before all this. I think I had a name."

Warrior: "Kyon Khryseos. The Golden Hound. I know."

He closes his eyes again. But this time it isn't fear.

Kyon: "...will you stay? Just for a moment."

Warrior: "As long as you need."

---

The nation rose when the warrior emerged from the cave — not with
a trophy, but with the truth. The story spread faster than Bastet
could contain it. Felines and canines, long divided, listened.
Some wept. Some raged. Most simply went quiet.

Bastet stood before the crowd that gathered at her gates. She did
not run. She faced them with the same composure she had always
carried — unbothered, unreadable. She looked at the canines whose
homes she had stripped away, whose lives she had unravelled for
the sake of a power she had wanted since the day she was exiled.

She spoke only once.

Bastet: "Was it I who betrayed you — or was it the people who
once swore to protect you?"

No one answered. The question hung in the air long after she
was gone.

---

With every prayer, every act of kindness, every moment two
species chose to understand each other rather than fear each
other — Kyon grew stronger. Slowly. Imperfectly.

The wounds were not forgotten. Not all canines accepted the
apology. Not all felines offered one.

But the cave was empty now, and the walls held the whole story
for anyone willing to descend and read them.

What does the future hold for this nation?

Perhaps that depends on who keeps walking forward.
